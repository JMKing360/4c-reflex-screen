The 4C Reflex Screen

Four reflexes.
One is running you right now.

Complaining. Criticizing. Comparing. Competing. Every person carries all four. One of them is louder than the others — and it is shaping your decisions, your relationships, and your unfinished work without your permission.

This screen takes under ten minutes. It shows you which reflex is most active, where it is showing up, and the three moves that interrupt it.

20 statements. Four options each.

Read each statement. Choose the option that is most true for you right now — not who you want to be. The more honest you are, the more useful the result.

There are no good or bad answers. There is only what is true.

Complaining
The Complaining Reflex

How your mind handles what is wrong.

I talk about what is wrong more than I act on what I can change.

When something goes wrong, my first move is to name the problem — not solve it.

I feel better after venting — but nothing actually changes.

I can list everything in my way. I have a harder time listing what I will do about it.

Criticizing
The Criticizing Reflex

How your mind handles your own performance.

My inner voice is harder on me than I would be on anyone I care about.

I speak to myself in ways I would not speak to someone I respect.

I rehearse my mistakes longer than I learn from them.

I see my flaws faster than I see what is working.

Comparing
The Comparing Reflex

How your mind handles other people's progress.

I measure my life against someone else's pace.

I scroll, then feel smaller than before I started.

I downgrade my own progress when I see someone else's.

I lose track of what I want when I see what they have.

Competing
The Competing Reflex

How your mind handles your own worth.

I track who is ahead of me in my field.

My sense of worth is tied to whether I am winning.

I do things mostly to be seen doing them.

I feel unsettled when someone in my circle succeeds at something I have not yet done.

Starting & Rhythm
Starting and Rhythm

How you begin and how you sustain.

I know what I need to do. Starting is the part that stalls me.

I start well. I lose the rhythm after a few days.

I do not have a clear way to measure whether I am actually growing.

I have no one who will notice — and say something — if I stop.
